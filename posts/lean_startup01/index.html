Summary: The Lean Startup by Eric Ries - Introduction

Posted on Jan 28, 2023

Summary

  • Writer talks about his own failure building a startup
  • A lesson from both of his successes and failure: its the boring stuff that matters the most - startup success can be engineered by following the right process - can be learned and can be taught
  • Entrepreneurship is a kind of management
  • A new entrepreneur movement built on many previous management and product development idea including: lean manufacturing, design thinking, customer development and agile development

Origins of the lean startup

  • Business and marketing functions of a startup are essential as engineering and product development aspect of a startup - deserves an equally rigorous methodology to guide them
  • Author mission: to improve the success rate of new innovative products, worldwide

The Lean Startup Method

Principles of the lean startup

Entrepreneurs are everywhere

  • Definition of entrepreneurship: anyone that works within a definition of a startup: human organisation that is formed to work on products or services under an extreme uncertainty
  • Entrepreneurs can be anywhere and the lean startup methodology is applicable to company or startup of any size, kinds and fields

Entrepreneurship is management

  • A startup is an institution - required a new kind of management to suit the uncertainty conditions arounds it
  • Entrepreneur should be defined as a role in every companies - work on innovation for future growth of a company

Validated learning

  • A startup is more than building product/service to cater for people needs - the aim is to build a sustainable business
  • To allow company to run experiment - to test each element of the vision

Build-measure-learn

  • The main activity of a startup is to turn ideas into products - measures how customer reacts and respond - learn from the feedback: to pivot or to preserve
  • Everything around a startup activities should be geared to accelerate this feedback loop

Innovation accounting

  • To focus on the boring stuff
  • Measure progress, set up milestones and how to prioritise works

Why startups failed

  • Traditional measurement of a startup/company success: a good plan, a solid strategy, through market research
  • The conditions surround a startup is full of uncertainty - its difficult to predict the future
  • Planning and forecasting are accurate when based on a long, stable operating history and a relatively static environment which most startups does not have
  • As some entrepreneurs and investors witnessed that traditional management or measurement failed to solve this problem - they just work hard to deliver whatever they think suits their project/startup
  • They theorised that “management is the problem, chaos must be the answer”

Management’s second century

  • Lean Startup movement is seeks to ensure that those of us who long to build the next big thing will have tools needed to change the world
